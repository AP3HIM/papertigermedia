2025 NFL Week 3 Predictions

Week 3 of the NFL season is upon us, and there are lots of interesting matchups. Will the Chiefs get their first win? Will the Colts stay hot? Will the Rams get their revenge on the Eagles? Let’s jump in and find out.

All rights go to the NFL and respective teams.

Bengals vs. Vikings

Jake Browning has proven himself as one of the league’s top backups, stepping in for Joe Burrow and delivering wins. Carson Wentz, on the other hand, hasn’t been a regular starter since 2022.

Records: Bengals (1-1), Vikings (0-2)
Key Stat: Minnesota is averaging just 14 PPG while allowing 27.5 PPG.

Final Score: Bengals 24, Vikings 10

Texans vs. Jaguars

The Texans (0-2) have lost heartbreakers — 14-9 to the Rams and 20-19 to the Bucs. The Jags (1-1) have more offensive balance and are at home.

Final Score: Jaguars 23, Texans 20

Colts vs. Titans

The Colts (2-0) are red hot — they’ve yet to punt this season — but red zone struggles remain. Rookie QB Cam Ward and the Titans (0-2) are searching for their first win. Divisional games are always close, but Indy has the momentum.

Final Score: Colts 27, Titans 24

Raiders vs. Commanders

Marcus Mariota takes over after Jayden Daniels’ injury. Washington (1-1) still has weapons, while the Raiders (0-2) have looked lost. Geno Smith’s 3-INT outing last week doesn’t inspire confidence in Las Vegas.

Final Score: Commanders 18, Raiders 6

Rams vs. Eagles

A rematch of last year’s Divisional Round, when the Eagles won 28-22 on the back of Saquon Barkley’s 205-yard day. The Rams (1-1) are hungry for revenge, and their defense has improved.

Final Score: Rams 32, Eagles 24

Falcons vs. Panthers

Bryce Young continues to struggle for Carolina (0-2). Atlanta (1-1) has looked sharper behind Michael Penix Jr., who has already thrown for 500+ yards in two weeks.

Final Score: Falcons 30, Panthers 10

Steelers vs. Patriots

A close matchup between Rodgers’ Steelers (1-1) and rookie QB Drake Maye’s Patriots (1-1). Pittsburgh’s defense has been uncharacteristically shaky, giving up 31 to Seattle last week.

Final Score: Patriots 22, Steelers 19

Packers vs. Browns

Green Bay (2-0) has looked dominant, beating Washington and Detroit in back-to-back weeks. The Browns (1-1) are feisty but lack the firepower to keep up.

Final Score: Packers 28, Browns 12

Jets vs. Buccaneers

Justin Fields is out with a concussion, so Tyrod Taylor takes over for the Jets (0-2). Tampa Bay (2-0) has shown resilience in tight wins, and they’ll make it three straight here.

Final Score: Buccaneers 27, Jets 14

Broncos vs. Chargers

The Broncos (0-2) lost a heartbreaker vs. Indy, while Bo Nix still shows rookie growing pains. The Chargers (1-1) defense under Jesse Minter looks legit.

Final Score: Chargers 28, Broncos 24

Saints vs. Seahawks

The Saints (0-2) fell to San Francisco despite Spencer Rattler’s solid 3-TD performance. Seattle (2-0) just put up 31 on Pittsburgh and looks balanced.

Final Score: Seahawks 34, Saints 17

Cowboys vs. Bears

Dak Prescott and Dallas (1-1) bounce back at home. The Bears (0-2) are still searching for answers with Ben Johnson as HC.

Final Score: Cowboys 38, Bears 14

Cardinals vs. 49ers

Mac Jones looked sharp for San Francisco (2-0), throwing 3 TDs against the Saints. The Cardinals (2-0) have squeaked out wins, but against weaker opponents.

Final Score: 49ers 29, Cardinals 17

Chiefs vs. Giants

The Chiefs (0-2) are in unfamiliar territory. The Giants (1-1) nearly upset Dallas last week and have Russell Wilson managing the game efficiently.

Final Score: Giants 27, Chiefs 24

Lions vs. Ravens

Detroit (1-1) is adjusting to new OC John Morton. Baltimore (2-0) has looked like legit contenders, led by Lamar Jackson’s 6 TDs through two games.

Final Score: Ravens 35, Lions 24

Conclusion
